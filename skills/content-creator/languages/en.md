## Hook Templates (Short-Form)

Use one verbatim, then customize. Each is tested across TikTok/Reels/Shorts/X.

- "I [unexpected action] for [timeframe]. Here's what changed."
- "[Common belief]? Wrong. Here's what actually happens."
- "Most [demographic] are doing [thing] backwards."
- "Three things changed everything. The third one most people miss."
- "If you're [demographic] and not doing [X], you're losing [Y]."
- "I deleted [thing]. My [metric] went up 40%."
- "Stop [common behavior]. Start [specific alternative]."
- "The [number] [thing] [target audience] never talks about."
- "Read this before you [action]."
- "I made every mistake so you don't have to. Here's the short list."
- "Why [respected name] does the opposite of [common advice]."
- "It took me [timeframe] to learn this. You can have it in 60 seconds."

## Tone Calibration

**Formality registers**
- **Bestie / casual**: contractions, sentence fragments, "you" / "ya'll" / "real talk"
- **Professional**: contractions OK, no fragments, "you" not "you guys"
- **Executive**: no contractions in headlines, no slang, precise verbs

**Idiom guidance**
- US-only idioms ("hit it out of the park", "Hail Mary", "Monday-morning quarterback") flatten outside North America. Default to plain language unless audience is US-specific.
- Avoid sports metaphors in B2B unless the brand has earned them.
- Cultural references age fast — "Netflix and chill" felt fresh in 2016, dated by 2020.

**Capitalization & punctuation norms**
- Headlines: sentence case beats Title Case for warmth ("Why we deleted half our codebase" > "Why We Deleted Half Our Codebase").
- Em dash for emphasis — like that. Not double hyphens. Not parentheses for the same beat.
- One exclamation point per piece, max. Zero is usually better.
- Oxford comma: keep it. Removing it can change meaning.
- Numbers: write 1–9 as words in body prose, 10+ as digits. In hooks, use digits always (they stop scrolls).

## Common English Mistakes (AI/translation tells)

- "Utilize" → use "use"
- "In order to" → "to"
- "Due to the fact that" → "because"
- "At this point in time" → "now"
- "It is important to note that" → just say the thing
- "Furthermore", "moreover", "additionally" stacked → cut, use periods
- Three-word taglines that mean nothing ("Innovate. Inspire. Transform.")
- "Solutions" as a euphemism for the actual product

## Cultural Reference Cautions

- US-centric holidays / sports / political references narrow your reach.
- "Class of [year]" framing is US-academic; international audiences may not parse.
- Currency: spell out USD if international; default to local currency in localized markets.
- Date formats: ISO (2026-04-30), US (April 30, 2026), UK (30 April 2026) — match audience.
- Measurements: imperial vs metric — match audience.
- Spelling: US (-ize, -or, -er) vs UK (-ise, -our, -re) — pick one and stay consistent.
