# Psychology Effects (18)

Pair one effect with a formula from `formulas.md`. Don't stack more than 2 in one piece — overload reads as manipulation.

---

## 1. Loss Aversion

**Mechanism** — losing $X hurts ~2× more than gaining $X feels good. Frame around what they're losing by not acting.

**Copy patterns**
- "You're already paying for [pain] — just not on an invoice."
- "Every month without [solution] costs [number]."

**Pairs well with** — PAS, BAB, PPPP.

**Pitfall** — fear-based stacking turns the brand into a bully. Use sparingly with high-trust verticals.

---

## 2. Scarcity

**Mechanism** — perceived rarity raises perceived value. Real scarcity beats fake every time.

**Copy patterns**
- "Only 47 left at this price."
- "Closes Friday at midnight — never reopened in 2 years."

**Pairs well with** — SLAP, Funnel-BOFU, Hook-Value-CTA.

**Pitfall** — fake countdowns get caught and torch trust. Only use real limits.

---

## 3. Social Proof

**Mechanism** — people copy what people like them are doing.

**Copy patterns**
- "12,000 [demographic] use it daily."
- "Your friend [name] just bought one."
- Pull-quote from a named customer (with photo if possible).

**Pairs well with** — ACC, FAB, 4Cs.

**Pitfall** — generic "everyone loves us" is invisible. Specific names and numbers convert.

---

## 4. Authority

**Mechanism** — credentials, affiliations, and recognized expertise compress evaluation time.

**Copy patterns**
- "Reviewed by [board-certified specialist]."
- "Featured in [tier-1 publication]."
- "Built by ex-[respected company] engineers."

**Pairs well with** — ACC, PPPP, Storytelling (founder origin).

**Pitfall** — borrowed authority that the audience doesn't recognize is dead weight.

---

## 5. Anchoring

**Mechanism** — the first number a reader sees shapes how they evaluate every subsequent number.

**Copy patterns**
- "Normally $499 — today $199."
- "Compare to [enterprise option] at $50K/year."
- "Saves you 14 hours/week — even at minimum wage that's $X."

**Pairs well with** — pricing pages, BOFU, FAB, BAB.

**Pitfall** — anchoring against a fake "MSRP" that no one ever paid breaks credibility.

---

## 6. FOMO

**Mechanism** — fear of being left out of an in-group experience.

**Copy patterns**
- "Everyone in [community] is already trying it."
- "Don't be the last team still using [old way]."
- Live-counter, "X people viewing now."

**Pairs well with** — SLAP, SSS, Hook-Value-CTA, launch announcements.

**Pitfall** — FOMO without an in-group reference falls flat. Name the tribe.

---

## 7. Future Pacing

**Mechanism** — vividly project the reader into the post-purchase outcome until they emotionally pre-commit.

**Copy patterns**
- "Imagine Sunday night — laptop closed, week already planned."
- "Picture the call where you tell [stakeholder] you hit the number."

**Pairs well with** — AIDA, PPPP, Storytelling.

**Pitfall** — generic future pacing ("imagine success!") is hollow. Use sensory specifics.

---

## 8. Curiosity Gap

**Mechanism** — open a loop the reader must close by continuing.

**Copy patterns**
- "The one ingredient I quit using changed everything."
- "We deleted half our codebase. Here's why revenue went up."
- "There's a reason the best [role]s never [common behavior]."

**Pairs well with** — Hook-Value-CTA, SSS, thread openers.

**Pitfall** — clickbait gap with thin payoff = trust collapse. Always honor the loop.

---

## 9. Decoy Effect

**Mechanism** — adding a deliberately unattractive third option makes the target option look obviously correct.

**Copy patterns**
- Pricing tables: Basic / **Pro** / Premium where Premium exists mainly to make Pro look like a steal.
- Bundle ladders: small / medium / **family** with family showing the best per-unit value.

**Pairs well with** — pricing pages, BOFU, FAB.

**Pitfall** — three plans that all look reasonable means no decoy at all.

---

## 10. Reciprocity

**Mechanism** — humans return value they receive — even unsolicited value.

**Copy patterns**
- "Free [audit/template/checklist] — no email required."
- "Here's the same playbook we charge $X for. On us."

**Pairs well with** — TOFU, MOFU lead magnets, ACC.

**Pitfall** — performative giving (gated "free" downloads, salesy follow-ups) breaks the trade.

---

## 11. Contrast

**Mechanism** — make the target look obvious by placing it next to a stark opposite.

**Copy patterns**
- Side-by-side comparison tables.
- "Old way: 3 weeks of meetings. New way: 1 doc."
- Before/after photos, dashboards, schedules.

**Pairs well with** — BAB, FAB, comparison pages.

**Pitfall** — exaggerated contrast that misrepresents the alternative invites pushback.

---

## 12. Commitment & Consistency

**Mechanism** — small early "yes"s make later "yes"s easier.

**Copy patterns**
- Multi-step quizzes with low-stakes early questions.
- "Tap if you've ever [pain]" before introducing the solution.
- Free trial → light onboarding → paid plan.

**Pairs well with** — funnel design, lead magnets, onboarding flows.

**Pitfall** — engineering false consistency (claiming the user already agreed) is manipulative.

---

## 13. Cognitive Ease

**Mechanism** — familiar fonts, simple words, smooth flow → "feels true." Friction → "feels suspicious."

**Copy patterns**
- 8th-grade reading level for cold audiences.
- Concrete nouns over abstract.
- Rhythmic line breaks; short sentences.

**Pairs well with** — every formula. Always apply this filter last.

**Pitfall** — dumbed-down for an expert audience reads as condescending. Match the register.

---

## 14. Risk Reversal

**Mechanism** — remove the perceived downside of saying yes.

**Copy patterns**
- "30-day money back, no questions."
- "Cancel anytime — even mid-month."
- "We'll migrate your data for free; if you don't love it, you keep it."

**Pairs well with** — PPPP, BOFU, finance/insurance/SaaS.

**Pitfall** — guarantee buried in T&Cs is worth zero. Lead with it.

---

## 15. Narrative Transport

**Mechanism** — when readers immerse in a story, they suspend critical scrutiny and accept embedded propositions.

**Copy patterns**
- First-person scenes with sensory detail.
- "I was sitting in [place] when [moment]…"
- Specific dialogue, named characters, real timestamps.

**Pairs well with** — Storytelling, BAB, founder posts.

**Pitfall** — abstract third-person "studies show" never transports. Get personal.

---

## 16. Pattern Interrupt

**Mechanism** — break the expected rhythm to reset attention.

**Copy patterns**
- One-word sentences after long ones. Like that.
- Unexpected visual or sound in the first frame.
- Counter-intuitive opening claim ("I quit my best customer last week.").

**Pairs well with** — Hook-Value-CTA, SLAP, SSS, Reels/Shorts hooks.

**Pitfall** — pattern interrupts everywhere = no pattern to interrupt.

---

## 17. Halo Effect

**Mechanism** — one strong positive trait spills over into perception of unrelated traits.

**Copy patterns**
- Celebrity / KOL / institutional endorsement.
- Premium packaging / design / typography that signals quality across the brand.
- "Used by [admired brand]" badges.

**Pairs well with** — Authority, Social Proof, FAB.

**Pitfall** — borrowing a halo you can't sustain (e.g., one celebrity post, no follow-through) creates dissonance.

---

## 18. Specificity Bias

**Mechanism** — precise numbers and specific names are perceived as more truthful than rounded or vague ones.

**Copy patterns**
- "Cuts onboarding from 14 days to 3" (not "faster onboarding").
- "Used by 1,247 teams" (not "thousands of teams").
- "Pulls from 6 sources every 90 seconds" (not "syncs in real time").

**Pairs well with** — every formula. Always reach for the precise number.

**Pitfall** — invented precision that doesn't survive scrutiny is worse than vagueness. Only specific if true.
