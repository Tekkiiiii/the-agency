# Copywriting Formulas (14)

Pick one as the spine of a piece, then layer 1–2 psychology effects from `psychology.md` on top. Combine 2 formulas only when each owns a different section (e.g., AIDA for the body, SLAP for the closer).

---

## 1. AIDA — Attention · Interest · Desire · Action

**Structure**
- **A**: pattern-interrupt opener that earns the next 3 seconds
- **I**: tie the topic to a problem the reader is already chewing on
- **D**: paint the future state — make them feel the outcome
- **A**: one clear CTA, no alternatives

**When to use** — long-form ads, landing pages, emotional sales pages, video scripts ≥30s.

**Example skeleton**
> Most [demographic] are [common but invisible loss]. ← A
> If [reader] keeps doing X, in 12 months they'll Y. ← I
> What changes when [outcome]? You finally [dream state described concretely]. ← D
> [Single CTA, action verb first.] ← A

**Pitfall** — bloated middle. Trim until each line earns its slot.

---

## 2. PAS — Problem · Agitate · Solution

**Structure**
- **P**: name the pain in the reader's exact words
- **A**: twist the knife — show what the pain costs over time, what it blocks, what it makes them feel
- **S**: position your offer as the specific relief

**When to use** — retargeting ads, problem-aware audiences, email reactivation, comment-bait posts.

**Example skeleton**
> You've tried X, Y, and Z — still [pain]. ← P
> Every week it gets worse: [stacking consequences]. ← A
> [Offer] is built for exactly this. Here's what changes by [timeframe]. ← S

**Pitfall** — agitating without earning trust = creepy. Earn the right to twist.

---

## 3. 4Cs — Clear · Concise · Compelling · Credible

**Structure** — four filters, not a sequence. Every line must pass all four.
- **Clear**: a 12-year-old understands it
- **Concise**: cut every word that isn't load-bearing
- **Compelling**: it would survive being read aloud
- **Credible**: backed by a number, name, or proof

**When to use** — product descriptions, listings, technical/B2B copy, anything where rational scrutiny is high.

**Example pass**
> Before: "Our cutting-edge solution leverages advanced AI to streamline workflows."
> After: "Closes 3 hours of manual ticket triage per agent per day. Used by [Company]."

**Pitfall** — "Compelling" gets ignored; copy becomes a spec sheet.

---

## 4. FAB — Features · Advantages · Benefits

**Structure**
- **F**: what it is / what it has
- **A**: what that lets the product do better than alternatives
- **B**: what that does for the reader's life or job

**When to use** — feature differentiation, comparison pages, competitor-switch pitches, mid-funnel content.

**Example**
> 256-bit on-device encryption (F)
> So your keys never leave the phone (A)
> Even if our servers are seized, your wallet stays yours. (B)

**Pitfall** — stopping at A. Always close the loop on B.

---

## 5. ACC — Agreement · Credibility · Call-to-action

**Structure**
- **Agreement**: open with a statement the reader already nods at
- **Credibility**: earn the right to keep talking via proof, credential, or precise observation
- **Call-to-action**: ask for the next small step

**When to use** — brand-building posts, thought-leadership, trust-first verticals (finance, health, B2B), founder-led content.

**Example**
> Most teams ship features no one uses. ← Agreement
> We rebuilt our roadmap last quarter using JTBD interviews; 4 of 6 launches now hit adoption goals. ← Credibility
> If you're stuck shipping into silence, here's the template we used → ← CTA

**Pitfall** — agreement that's so universal it sounds AI-generated. Be specific.

---

## 6. SLAP — Stop · Look · Act · Purchase

**Structure**
- **Stop**: kinetic visual or shock line that interrupts the scroll
- **Look**: 1–2 lines that explain what they're seeing
- **Act**: tell them what to do right now
- **Purchase**: remove every barrier between intent and checkout

**When to use** — cold short-form ads, bumper ads, in-feed promo, flash sales, story stickers.

**Example**
> "Don't buy [X] this month." ← Stop
> Why? Because the new version drops in 9 days and current owners get it free. ← Look
> Pre-order now → ← Act
> [One-tap checkout, saved payment.] ← Purchase

**Pitfall** — front-loaded shock with no payoff. The "Look" must justify the "Stop".

---

## 7. BAB — Before · After · Bridge

**Structure**
- **Before**: the painful current state, described in sensory detail
- **After**: the desired future state, described in sensory detail
- **Bridge**: the specific path your offer provides between them

**When to use** — transformation stories, case studies, testimonial-driven ads, switch-from-competitor pitches.

**Example**
> Before: 11 PM, third coffee, copying numbers between 6 spreadsheets.
> After: 4 PM, dashboard already updated, walking out the door.
> Bridge: [Tool] auto-syncs the same 6 sources in 90 seconds. Setup takes one afternoon.

**Pitfall** — symmetric Before/After (same length, same depth) feels engineered. Let one breathe longer.

---

## 8. 5W1H — What · Why · Who · When · Where · How

**Structure** — answer all six in the order most relevant to the reader's first question.

**When to use** — product listings, catalog copy, FAQ sections, event announcements, press-release lite.

**Example**
> **What**: 14-day pop-up for [collection]
> **Why**: limited run; not restocking
> **Who**: [audience]
> **When**: opens [date], ends [date]
> **Where**: [link / location]
> **How**: [process — order, ship, claim]

**Pitfall** — robotic delivery. Weave the six into prose; don't always print the labels.

---

## 9. Storytelling — Character · Conflict · Resolution · Result

**Structure**
- **Character**: protagonist the reader recognizes themselves in
- **Conflict**: the specific moment when it stopped working
- **Resolution**: the decision/turn — what they tried and why it stuck
- **Result**: what changed, with one number or one observable artifact

**When to use** — long-form posts, founder narratives, customer stories, brand films, podcast intros.

**Example**
> Lan ran her studio's books in a notebook for three years. ← Character
> Then her best client paid the wrong invoice — twice. ← Conflict
> She moved to [tool] over a single Saturday. ← Resolution
> Six months later, no invoice errors and 40% more billable hours captured. ← Result

**Pitfall** — too much "Character" before any "Conflict". Get to the trouble fast.

---

## 10. SSS — Short · Simple · Shareable

**Structure** — three constraints applied to every draft.
- **Short**: cut to the bone; one idea per piece
- **Simple**: one sentence per beat; no clauses
- **Shareable**: line that someone would screenshot or quote

**When to use** — Reels/Shorts captions, TikTok hooks, Threads, X posts, comment-bait.

**Example**
> Stop optimizing your morning routine.
> Optimize what you do at 4 PM. That's where days are won.

**Pitfall** — "shareable" mistaken for "edgy". Aim for true, not loud.

---

## 11. Hook · Value · CTA

**Structure**
- **Hook**: opening line/frame that earns the next scroll-second
- **Value**: the actual takeaway — concrete, useful in <10 seconds
- **CTA**: single action, frictionless

**When to use** — every short-form caption (TikTok, Reels, Shorts, Facebook), thread openers.

**Example**
> Hook: "I deleted 80% of my calendar last month."
> Value: "Three categories of meeting were doing nothing: status updates, FYI demos, and 'syncs.' I replaced each with a Loom or a doc."
> CTA: "Comment 'CALENDAR' and I'll send the template."

**Pitfall** — hook over-promises, value under-delivers. People learn fast; they won't return.

---

## 12. PPPP — Picture · Promise · Prove · Push

**Structure**
- **Picture**: paint the desired outcome vividly
- **Promise**: state plainly what your offer delivers toward that picture
- **Prove**: evidence — numbers, customers, demos, audits
- **Push**: ask for the action with a defined deadline or limit

**When to use** — sales pages, pitch decks, webinars, high-consideration B2B and high-ticket B2C.

**Example**
> Picture: shipping a 4-week product on a 1-week timeline.
> Promise: [tool] generates the boilerplate, the tests, and the deploy script.
> Prove: 12,000 teams; G2 4.8; case study: [Company] ↓ time-to-prod 73%.
> Push: $0 for the first month if you start before Friday.

**Pitfall** — "Prove" that's vague. One specific number beats five vague ones.

---

## 13. Funnel — TOFU · MOFU · BOFU

**Structure** — pick the stage, then choose formula.
- **TOFU** (awareness): broad pain or category education → Storytelling, ACC, SSS
- **MOFU** (consideration): comparison, JTBD, anti-objection → BAB, FAB, 4Cs
- **BOFU** (decision): proof, risk reversal, last push → PPPP, PAS, SLAP

**When to use** — building a campaign across multiple touches; mapping a content calendar.

**Example mapping for one product launch**
- TOFU: 3 Storytelling reels on the problem space
- MOFU: 1 BAB long-form + comparison page
- BOFU: PPPP sales page + PAS retargeting ad

**Pitfall** — running the same formula across all stages.

---

## 14. COC — Cluster of Content (Pillar → Micro)

**Structure**
- **Pillar**: one long-form anchor — essay, video, podcast, deck (≥10 min equivalent)
- **Micro**: chop into 8–20 short pieces tuned to each platform's format

**When to use** — when one strong idea needs to cover a quarter of content; when the team is small.

**Example**
> Pillar: 30-min YouTube on "The 4-step content framework."
> Micro:
>   - 4 TikToks (one per step, 45s each, SSS + Hook-Value-CTA)
>   - 1 carousel per step (Instagram + LinkedIn)
>   - 5 X posts (best lines from the script)
>   - 1 newsletter (BAB summary linking to the video)

**Pitfall** — copy-paste micros across platforms. Each platform needs its native format from `references/platforms.md`.
