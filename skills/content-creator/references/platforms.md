# Platform Constraints & Conventions

Read only the section(s) for platforms named in the brief. Each section gives format limits, hook windows, native CTAs, and a "what wins right now" note. Update as platform conventions shift.

---

## TikTok

**Format**
- Vertical 9:16, 15s–3min (sweet spot 21–60s)
- Caption: ~2,200 chars (only first ~100 visible before "more")
- 3–5 hashtags, mix broad + niche

**Hook window** — first 1.5 seconds. Lose them = lost.

**Native CTAs** — "Comment [keyword]", "Save this for later", "Follow for part 2", "Tap the link in bio"

**What wins**
- Pattern Interrupt opener (visual or claim) before the brand even appears
- Text overlay reinforces the hook for sound-off viewers
- Trending sound (within 7-day window)
- "Part 1 of N" sequels for retention
- Comment-bait questions in caption

**Avoid**
- Logo or product shot in frame 1
- Slow burn intros — by second 3 you've lost half
- Watermarks from CapCut, Reels, etc.

## Instagram (Reels + Feed)

**Format**
- Reels: vertical 9:16, 15–90s (best 15–30s for completion)
- Feed image/carousel: 1:1 or 4:5
- Carousel: 2–10 slides; first slide is the hook
- Caption: ~2,200 chars; first ~125 visible

**Hook window** — first 2 seconds (Reels) or first slide (carousel)

**Native CTAs** — "Save this", "Share to your story", "Comment [emoji]", "DM us [keyword]", "Link in bio"

**What wins**
- Carousels for educational content (high save rate)
- Reels for reach beyond followers
- Text-on-image hook front-loaded
- Aesthetic consistency (color, font) across grid
- 3–5 niche hashtags, no banned ones

**Avoid**
- Recycled TikToks with watermark
- Engagement bait that violates Meta's policy ("comment X to win")
- Long captions on Reels (people don't read)

## Facebook

**Format**
- Posts: 1:1 image or 16:9 video
- Reels: 9:16 (same as IG)
- Carousel ads: up to 10 cards
- Caption: 63,206 chars max but cut off at ~80 words

**Hook window** — first sentence and first 1.3 seconds of video

**Native CTAs** — "Click to learn more", "Send a message", "Shop now", "Comment below"

**What wins**
- Long-form storytelling (audience skews older, reads more)
- Local community angle (groups, neighborhoods, families)
- High-trust signals (reviews, named customers)
- Comments-as-content (replies build the algorithm signal)
- Boosting good organic posts is cheaper than starting from cold

**Avoid**
- Multi-link posts (algo punishes)
- Pure outbound link without context
- TikTok-native trends (audience is 5+ years older on average)

## LinkedIn

**Format**
- Text post: 3,000 chars; first 3 lines visible before "see more"
- Document carousel (PDF): 8–12 pages, 4:5 ratio
- Native video: 30s–10min, 16:9 or 1:1
- Polls: 24h–14d duration
- Newsletter: longer-form, recurring cadence

**Hook window** — first 3 lines of text post (the "see more" gate)

**Native CTAs** — "Comment [letter] for the playbook", "Repost if useful", "DM me", "Follow for more"

**What wins**
- Personal narratives with concrete numbers and named companies
- Document carousels for frameworks/checklists (high save + share)
- Anti-conventional-wisdom takes (with receipts)
- Founder/exec voice over brand handle
- Reply to every comment in first hour (algorithm boost)

**Avoid**
- "I'm humbled to announce" (parodied to death)
- Outbound link in main body (drop in first comment instead)
- Stock photos (use real screenshots/photos)
- Emoji bullet lists ✅✅✅ (read as low-effort)

## YouTube Shorts

**Format**
- Vertical 9:16, ≤60s
- Title: 100 chars; appears overlaid on video
- Description: 5,000 chars but mostly invisible

**Hook window** — first 2 seconds, plus a "loop hook" at the end to drive replays

**Native CTAs** — "Subscribe for more", "Watch the full video", "Comment your answer"

**What wins**
- Tight loop endings (visually return to frame 1)
- Series format ("Day X of building Y")
- Subtitles/captions burned in
- Specific niche topic > generic lifestyle

**Avoid**
- Content optimized for TikTok's algorithm (different signals)
- Long intros — Shorts is unforgiving

## X / Twitter

**Format**
- Single post: 280 chars (Premium: 4,000)
- Thread: 5–25 posts, each 280 chars
- Image: 16:9 or 1:1 native
- Video: 2:20 max (Premium longer)

**Hook window** — first post of a thread; image overlay if attached

**Native CTAs** — "Bookmark this", "Reply with [emoji]", "RT if you've felt this", "Click for the full thread"

**What wins**
- Threads with one idea per post + a strong "1/" hook
- Quote-tweet commentary on a notable post
- Replying to viral posts in your niche (visibility hack)
- Specificity and contrarian takes
- Image with text overlay (people stop on it)

**Avoid**
- Long threads with weak first post — only the first is judged
- "Here's a thread 🧵" without immediate value
- Outbound links in the first post (algorithm penalizes)

## Threads

**Format**
- Post: 500 chars
- Image, video, multi-image
- Replies are expanded inline (long conversational threads work)

**Hook window** — entire post is visible inline; less of a "first line" gate than X

**Native CTAs** — softer than X; conversational prompts ("what do you think?", "anyone else?")

**What wins**
- Casual, conversational tone (more like a group chat)
- Asking questions and replying often
- Light snark > polished thought leadership
- Cross-posted from IG, but tonally adjusted

**Avoid**
- Direct port from LinkedIn (too formal)
- Hard-sell CTAs (the audience is in browsing mode)

## Farcaster

**Format**
- Cast: 320 chars (recently expanded; assume 320)
- Long-cast: longer; check current limit
- Frames: interactive embeds (transactions, polls, mini-apps)

**Hook window** — first line; web3-native context

**Native CTAs** — "Mint this", "Follow [channel]", "Cast your reply", Frame interactions

**What wins**
- Transparency about onchain activity (proof, addresses)
- Channel-specific posting (e.g., /dev, /design, /founders)
- Tipping behavior reciprocates engagement
- Real-time crypto/builder commentary

**Avoid**
- Web2 marketing-speak
- Posting to feed without channel targeting
- Hard-sell tokens (community will dunk)

## Email

**Format**
- Subject line: ~50 chars (mobile cuts at ~30)
- Preheader: ~85 chars
- Body: scannable; one idea per email; mobile-first
- Plain-text often outperforms HTML for cold/nurture

**Hook window** — subject + preheader. Open or die.

**Native CTAs** — single button, single link, action verb ("Start the trial", "Read the breakdown")

**What wins**
- Personal "from a person" sender name
- Single CTA per email
- Subject lines that look like a friend's email, not a brand's
- P.S. line that reinforces the CTA
- Segmented sends (cold ≠ active)

**Avoid**
- Multiple CTAs (cuts conversion ~30%)
- "Newsletter" framing (down-engages from "an actual message")
- HTML-heavy templates for cold outreach
- Subject lines that promise X but deliver Y

## Blog / SEO

**Format**
- Title: 50–60 chars (SEO + click-through)
- Meta description: 150–160 chars
- Body: 800–2,500 words (varies by intent)
- H1 once, H2/H3 nested logically
- Schema: FAQ, HowTo, Article where relevant

**Hook window** — title + first paragraph (above the fold)

**Native CTAs** — embedded in content ("Try it free", inline product mentions), end-of-post CTA, sticky sidebar

**What wins**
- Match search intent precisely (informational vs commercial vs transactional)
- Original data, screenshots, demos — not regurgitated lists
- Internal links to related posts and product pages
- Update existing high-ranking posts > publishing new ones
- Featured snippet structure (definition, list, table)

**Avoid**
- AI-template intros ("In today's fast-paced world…")
- Keyword stuffing
- Listicle without depth in each item
- Misleading title vs body content
