# Voice Tones (6)

Pick one tone per piece. Mixing tones in a single draft is the single most common reason copy reads "off." Presets and language packs override the default tone for niche/locale fit.

---

## 1. Friendly Bestie

**Audience fit** — peer-to-peer DTC, consumer apps, lifestyle brands, beauty/cosmetics, fashion, F&B; works for ages 16–35 across cultures.

**Sample sentences**
- "Okay listen — this is the one I keep recommending."
- "Real talk: I almost returned it. Then day 4 happened."
- "If you've been on the fence, this is the sign."

**Do**
- Use second-person heavily ("you", "your")
- Drop in colloquial connectors: "okay", "listen", "real talk", "ngl"
- Reference shared cultural moments

**Don't**
- Use jargon, certifications, technical specs (kills the warmth)
- Start with "Introducing…" or "We're excited to…"
- Switch into formal mid-paragraph

---

## 2. Confident Expert

**Audience fit** — B2B, professional services, finance, health, advanced practitioner audiences who respect competence.

**Sample sentences**
- "After auditing 240 of these, here's what consistently breaks."
- "Three things matter here. The other twelve are theatre."
- "If your [metric] is below [threshold], the issue is almost always [cause]."

**Do**
- Lead with observations, numbers, or counter-intuitive claims
- Use specific data points and named tools/concepts
- Be willing to disagree with conventional wisdom

**Don't**
- Hedge ("might", "could possibly", "in some cases")
- Self-promote ("we're industry-leading")
- Use exclamation marks (kills credibility)

---

## 3. Trusted Advisor

**Audience fit** — finance, insurance, healthcare, legal, high-stakes B2B, anything where the buyer is anxious about getting it wrong.

**Sample sentences**
- "Before you decide, here are the three numbers that matter most."
- "You don't need to act today. You do need to know this."
- "The honest answer is: it depends on [factor]. Here's how to tell which side you're on."

**Do**
- Acknowledge complexity; don't oversimplify
- Surface the trade-offs, not just the upside
- Cite credentials and regulators by name when relevant

**Don't**
- Hype ("game-changing", "revolutionary")
- Push urgency on regulated products
- Promise outcomes you can't guarantee

---

## 4. Builder-to-Builder

**Audience fit** — developer tools, devops, infra, technical SaaS, dev advocacy, engineering-led brands.

**Sample sentences**
- "We rewrote the parser. Here's the diff and the perf delta."
- "Yeah, you could roll your own. We did. It cost us 6 months."
- "If you're hitting [error], the fix is usually [thing]. If not, here's the deeper dive."

**Do**
- Show, don't tell — code, screenshots, dashboards, command output
- Acknowledge the obvious DIY alternative and why your tool wins
- Be willing to point at competitors by name

**Don't**
- Marketing-speak ("seamless integration", "best-in-class")
- Hide behind jargon to seem smart — clarity is the flex
- Skip the "why" — engineers want to understand, not just consume

---

## 5. Inspirational Leader

**Audience fit** — purpose-driven brands, founder voices, community/movement building, education, ESG, mission-led B2B.

**Sample sentences**
- "We didn't build this to win an industry. We built it because [problem] keeps people awake."
- "The next ten years of [field] will be decided by people who [behavior]. We're building for them."
- "What if [aspirational reframe]?"

**Do**
- Anchor in mission and "why now"
- Use concrete examples to keep aspiration from drifting into platitude
- Tie individual action to a larger arc

**Don't**
- Drift into hollow platitudes ("change the world", "be the difference")
- Skip the proof — vision without evidence reads as performative
- Talk about the brand more than the reader

---

## 6. Playful / Whimsical

**Audience fit** — consumer brands seeking differentiation, mobile games, novelty products, kid/family brands, brands targeting Gen Z, indie SaaS with personality.

**Sample sentences**
- "It's a calendar. It's a confessional. It's mostly a calendar."
- "We made the boring thing slightly less boring. You're welcome."
- "Built for people who name their houseplants."

**Do**
- Be specific in the joke — vague humor reads as forced quirk
- Earn the play with a real product benefit underneath
- Use surprising imagery and unexpected line breaks

**Don't**
- Be playful in regulated verticals (finance, health, crypto compliance copy)
- Joke at the audience's expense
- Stack jokes — one good line beats five mediocre ones
