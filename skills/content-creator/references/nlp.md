# NLP Copywriting Techniques (10)

These are linguistic micro-patterns that bypass surface scrutiny. Use 1–2 per piece. Stacking five reads as cult-y.

---

## 1. Presupposition

**Mechanism** — embed a fact inside a question or claim so the reader accepts it implicitly to engage with the sentence.

**Patterns**
- "When you start [outcome], you'll notice [detail] first." → presupposes they will start.
- "The next time [pain happens], remember [reframe]." → presupposes pain will recur.
- "Once you switch, your team will…" → presupposes switching.

**Pitfall** — too aggressive ("when you buy our $5K plan…") triggers reactance.

---

## 2. Embedded Command

**Mechanism** — wrap an instruction inside a longer sentence so it lands without the reader's defenses going up. Often emphasized via formatting.

**Patterns**
- "Most teams find that *they save 6 hours a week* once they install it."
- "You don't have to *book a demo today* — but the next slot is two weeks out."

**Pitfall** — heavy-handed bolding makes the manipulation visible. Subtle.

---

## 3. Open Loop

**Mechanism** — pose a question or set up a thread, then delay the resolution. The reader's brain holds attention until closure.

**Patterns**
- "There's one mistake I made that cost us $40K. I'll get to it. First…"
- Thread opener: "Three things changed everything. The third one most people miss."

**Pairs well with** — Curiosity Gap, long-form posts, video scripts.

**Pitfall** — open loops you never close train the audience to scroll past.

---

## 4. Pacing & Leading

**Mechanism** — first state observable truths the reader already agrees with (pacing), then propose your conclusion (leading). The agreement momentum carries them.

**Patterns**
- "It's Friday. You're tired. Your inbox still has 47 unread. → That's not a productivity problem — it's an interface problem."

**Pairs well with** — ACC, Storytelling, B2B founder posts.

**Pitfall** — three pace lines, then a leap that doesn't actually follow. The lead has to earn the agreements.

---

## 5. Sensory Language

**Mechanism** — concrete sensory words (visual, auditory, kinesthetic, gustatory) make abstract claims feel real.

**Patterns**
- Visual: "Watch the dashboard turn green."
- Auditory: "The room goes quiet when you say the number."
- Kinesthetic: "Smooth, weightless, no drag on your skin."
- Gustatory: "Bright citrus opening, then cedar."

**Pairs well with** — Future Pacing, Storytelling, BAB.

**Pitfall** — sensory salad without grounding (mixing 4 modalities in 2 lines) feels like ad copy.

---

## 6. Double Bind

**Mechanism** — offer two options, both of which lead to the desired action.

**Patterns**
- "Want to start with the free trial or jump straight to the team plan?"
- "Should I send you the playbook now, or wait until Monday morning?"

**Pairs well with** — checkout pages, sales DMs, onboarding.

**Pitfall** — false binary the reader sees through ("yes I want it / yes I really want it") feels insulting. Both options must be reasonable.

---

## 7. Power Words

**Mechanism** — high-imagery, high-emotion words that punch above their weight per syllable. Specific lists live in each language pack.

**Categories**
- Curiosity: secret, hidden, unknown, behind-the-scenes
- Urgency: now, today, before, last
- Trust: proven, guaranteed, certified, audited
- Sensory: silky, sharp, crisp, raw
- Identity: maker, founder, operator, parent

**Pairs well with** — every formula. Apply during the final polish pass.

**Pitfall** — every line a power word = no power words. One per ~3 sentences.

---

## 8. Identity Labeling

**Mechanism** — name the reader as a member of an aspirational group; they'll act consistently with the label.

**Patterns**
- "Founders who actually ship use [tool]."
- "If you're the kind of [role] who hates wasted meetings…"
- "Smart [demographic] are switching to [option]."

**Pairs well with** — Storytelling, ACC, FAB, FOMO.

**Pitfall** — labels that flatter without substance ("you're a winner!") read as cheap.

---

## 9. Negative Future Pacing

**Mechanism** — vividly project the reader into the cost of inaction, the inverse of standard future pacing.

**Patterns**
- "Picture this same conversation, six months from now, with the same numbers."
- "If nothing changes, [pain] gets ~30% worse compounding monthly."

**Pairs well with** — PAS, Loss Aversion, BOFU, finance.

**Pitfall** — fear porn without an exit ramp. Always pivot to the solution.

---

## 10. Social Currency

**Mechanism** — give the reader something to share that signals smart/in-the-know status to their group.

**Patterns**
- "The framework none of your competitors are using yet."
- Quotable one-liners ("Optimize your 4 PM, not your 6 AM.")
- Insider data ("Avg [metric] across 2,400 teams: [number].")

**Pairs well with** — Storytelling, SSS, Curiosity Gap, thought leadership.

**Pitfall** — content that's only valuable to share, not to use. The reader notices fast.
