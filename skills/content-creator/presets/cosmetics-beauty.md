# Preset — Cosmetics & Beauty

Loaded only when `preset=cosmetics-beauty`. Layers niche-specific angles, hooks, trust accelerators, pricing patterns, and compliance reminders on top of the framework. Works with any language pack and any demographic.

---

## Niche Coverage

- **Skincare** — cleansers, toners, serums, moisturizers, masks, sunscreens, treatments (acne, anti-aging, brightening)
- **Makeup** — base (foundation, concealer, powder), eye, lip, complexion (blush, bronzer, highlighter), tools
- **Fragrance** — EDP/EDT/parfum, body mists, layered scents
- **Haircare** — shampoo, conditioner, treatments, tools, styling, scalp care
- **Body care** — body wash, lotion, scrub, slugging, hand/foot
- **Wellness-adjacent beauty** — supplements (skin/hair/nails), oral care, intimate care

---

## Audience Archetypes

- **Skin-concern-driven** — has a specific problem (acne, melasma, sensitivity, aging). Reads ingredient labels. Wants efficacy proof.
- **Ritual-driven** — finds joy in the routine itself. Buys for sensory experience and aesthetic.
- **Trend-driven** — chases TikTok-viral and KOL-recommended drops. Short consideration window.
- **Ingredient-nerd** — researches actives, % concentration, formulation. Suspicious of marketing fluff. Won over by transparency.
- **Minimalist** — wants 3-step routine max. Multi-tasking products win.
- **Luxury-positional** — buys for brand prestige and packaging. Performance is necessary but not sufficient.

Match angle and tone to archetype before drafting.

## Beauty-Specific Hooks

**Confession openers** (high engagement)
- "I had [skin concern] for [timeframe] until I quit [common product]."
- "Mình từng nghĩ da mình hết cứu — cho đến khi…"
- "PSA: stop using [popular product] if you have [skin condition]."

**Ingredient-curiosity gaps**
- "Why every dermatologist quietly uses [ingredient]."
- "The active that fixed my [concern] in 6 weeks. (And it costs $14.)"
- "If your serum doesn't have [ingredient], it's not doing what you think."

**Visual transformation**
- "Day 1 vs Day 30. No filter. No edits."
- "Da mình sau 4 tuần — không filter, không chỉnh."

**Anti-trend takes**
- "Stop double-cleansing if [condition]."
- "The 7-step routine that wrecked my barrier."

**Application/texture demos**
- Open with the texture moment (silky pump, melting cream, mist spray) — sound on.

## Trust Accelerators

Stack 2–3 per piece for high-consideration purchases:

- **Clinical %**: "94% saw [outcome] after 4 weeks (clinical, n=120)" — must be real data, source available.
- **Before/after timeframes**: photos with day counts, lighting controlled.
- **Ingredient transparency**: full INCI list, % of actives shown.
- **Dermatologist / pharmacist credentials**: name, board, location.
- **KOL/KOC partnerships**: long-term ambassadors > one-off posts.
- **Third-party testing**: dermatologist-tested, allergy-tested, fragrance-free certified.
- **Awards / press**: "Allure Best of Beauty," "Cosmopolitan UK Editor's Pick," "Đẹp Magazine Award."
- **Real customer count**: "Used by 28,000+ skincare devotees in [country]."
- **Refill / sustainability**: refillable packaging, glass jars, certified clean.

Avoid borrowed authority you can't sustain (one celebrity post, no follow-up).

## Pricing Patterns

- **Starter / Full-Size / Bundle ladder** — minis at $X to lower trial friction; full size at 2.5×; bundle at 4× with the highest per-mL value.
- **Gift-with-purchase** — minis or branded merch at the $50/$80/$120 thresholds.
- **Subscription replenishment** — 10–15% off on monthly/quarterly auto-ship for consumables (cleansers, sunscreen).
- **Mini-size sampling** — sachets in shipping; deluxe minis on landing page.
- **Bundles by routine** — "AM routine," "PM routine," "Acne routine" — solves choice fatigue.
- **Tier vs Hero** — one obvious "must-buy" hero SKU + supporting cast (Decoy Effect on lower SKUs).
- **Local market pricing** — VN: avoid round numbers like 999K (charm), use 1.099K-style (specificity); US: charm pricing $X9.99 still works DTC.

## Voice Tone Defaults

- **Friendly Bestie** (peer-to-peer, social-first brands)
- **Confident Expert with Warmth** (clinical/derm-positioned brands — never cold-clinical)
- **Inspirational Leader** (purpose/sustainability-led brands)

Avoid: pure clinical-cold (alienates 25–35 women), pure inspirational-vague (no proof to back), forced quirk (reads as desperate).

## Compliance Reminders

**Always surface these with the draft.** Cosmetics regulation is strict and varies by market.

**Universal**
- Never claim to "cure," "treat," "prevent," or "diagnose" any condition — those are drug claims.
- "Anti-aging" is acceptable in most markets; "reverses aging" is not.
- Substantiate every numerical claim ("97% reduction in…") with on-file evidence.
- Disclose paid partnerships (#ad, #sponsored, [tài trợ], [hợp tác trả phí]).

**Vietnam (`language=vi`)**
- Bộ Y Tế / Cục An Toàn Thực Phẩm rules: cosmetic products may not claim medicinal effect. Forbidden: "chữa khỏi," "miễn nhiễm," "100% hết [bệnh]", "thay thế thuốc."
- Use: "hỗ trợ giảm," "cải thiện rõ rệt," "giúp da [outcome]"
- Cosmetic registration number (số công bố mỹ phẩm) should be available on request for any claim-heavy ad.

**United States (`language=en`)**
- FDA cosmetic-vs-drug claim line: any claim that affects body structure/function = drug claim, requires FDA approval.
- "Clinical" claims: substantiation must be on file (FTC).
- FTC endorsement guides apply to influencer/KOL content.

**EU**
- Regulation (EC) No 1223/2009 — claims must be supported by evidence; CPNP notification required.
- Common safe phrasings: "helps," "supports," "appears to."

**Always add to the draft**: "Note: cosmetic claims require evidence on file. Confirm regulatory compliance for [target market] before publishing."
