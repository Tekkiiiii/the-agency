Loaded only when `preset=crypto`. Layers crypto-specific angles, hooks, trust accelerators, pricing patterns, and **very strict** compliance reminders on top of the framework.

## Crypto-Specific Hooks

**For traders / degens**
- "TVL just crossed $X. Here's what changed in the last 7 days."
- "Three onchain signals nobody is talking about."
- "Don't get rekt by [specific risk]. Here's how to check."
- "I tracked [whale wallet] for 30 days. Here's the playbook."
- "Why we switched from [protocol A] to [protocol B]."

**For builders**
- "We deployed [contract type] on [chain]. Gas + settlement breakdown."
- "Audit report just landed. Here's what surprised us."
- "If you're building on [chain], you probably don't need [common pattern]."

**For normies**
- "Crypto explained without the jargon: [topic] in 90 seconds."
- "Three mistakes new wallet users make in week one."
- "What [bank] won't tell you about stablecoins."

**For governance**
- "[Protocol] vote closes in 48h. Here's what's on the ballot and what it means."
- "Governance update: [change] passed. Here's what changes for you."

**For launches / ecosystem**
- "[Protocol] mainnet is live. The 3 things that work today."
- "The airdrop everyone's talking about — eligibility math."

**Avoid hooks built around**: "next 100x," "guaranteed yield," "moon," "WAGMI" (alone, without substance).

## Trust Accelerators

Required because crypto trust starts at zero:

- **Audit reports** — link to Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin, Certik, Spearbit, Code4rena reports. Reference the date and version audited.
- **On-chain proof** — link to verified contract, Etherscan/Solscan, on-chain TVL dashboards (DefiLlama).
- **Proof-of-Reserves** — for CEXes; show the methodology, signed messages, audit attestations.
- **Investor / backer logos** — name reputable funds (Paradigm, a16z, Variant, Polychain) — only with permission/public info.
- **Dev-team doxx status** — fully doxxed, partially doxxed, or anon — be transparent.
- **Multisig + timelock** — disclose sig schemes (e.g., Gnosis Safe 5-of-7, 48h timelock) for protocol upgrades.
- **Bug bounty program** — Immunefi tier, max payout, history of payouts.
- **Open-source repo** — GitHub link, recent activity, contributor count.
- **Time-in-market** — "Live since [date], $X TVL ATH, $Y total volume settled."
- **Custody clarity** — non-custodial vs custodial, where keys live, who controls upgrades.

Avoid: vague "audited" without naming auditor + date; "secure" claims without specifics; quoting CT influencers as endorsement.

## Pricing Patterns

- **Zero-fee trading hooks** — common for CEX promos; be explicit about the maker/taker tier afterward.
- **Gas optimization claims** — show benchmark vs alternative ("L2 gas: $0.04 vs Ethereum mainnet: $14.20").
- **Staking APR ladders** — show base rate, boosted rate, and lockup/unlock conditions. Disclose variability.
- **Points / airdrop programs** — be honest that points are not tokens until TGE (token generation event).
- **Fee rebates for stakers / VIPs** — show the tier table.
- **Variable APRs** — emphasize "rate may change with market conditions" — never imply fixed yield.
- **Subsidized launch incentives** — disclose duration and source ("incentives from foundation through Q3 2026").

Never present APR/yield as if it's fixed unless it's actually fixed (even then, disclose source and counterparty risk).

## Compliance Reminders — VERY STRICT

**Every draft must include the legal-review + jurisdictional flag at the bottom.** Crypto compliance varies wildly by country and changes monthly.

**Universal rules**
- Never call a token a "good investment," "guaranteed yield," or "risk-free."
- Never imply outcomes without disclaimers ("returns vary," "past performance ≠ future results").
- Distinguish between retail and institutional content; never market institutional-only products to retail.
- Disclose paid partnerships (#ad, #sponsored, paid collab, partner-funded post).
- Avoid testimonials about returns — they're regulated as financial promotions in many markets.
- Never use a celebrity endorsement without legal sign-off (SEC has prosecuted these).
- For exchanges/CEXes: clearly distinguish between regions where you are licensed vs not.
- Never claim "compliant" without naming the regulator + framework + license number.
- Open-source code license + on-chain immutability claims must be accurate.

**United States**
- **SEC** — securities laws apply to many tokens, ICOs, NFTs (Howey test). Solicitation of unregistered securities is illegal.
- **CFTC** — derivatives (perps, futures) jurisdiction
- **FinCEN** — BSA/AML for money transmitters
- **State Money Transmitter Licenses** — required state-by-state
- **OFAC** — sanctions screening, geo-blocking
- **NY BitLicense** — required for activity in NY
- **California DFPI** — DFAL applies to digital asset businesses
- **Howey Test risk** — almost any "expectation of profit from efforts of others" framing creates exposure

**EU**
- **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)** — full framework for CASPs (crypto-asset service providers); marketing communications regulated
- **MiCA whitepaper** required for many token offerings
- **AMLD6** — KYC/AML
- Country-level: BaFin (DE), AMF (FR), CySEC (CY), MFSA (MT)

**United Kingdom**
- **FCA** — crypto promotions are regulated financial promotions; require either FCA authorization or s21 exemption
- Strict cooling-off periods, risk warnings, "don't invest unless you can afford to lose" disclaimers required
- Memecoins and NFTs: case-by-case classification

**Singapore**
- **MAS** — Payment Services Act (PSA) for DPT services
- Marketing to retail is heavily restricted; can't advertise DPT services in public spaces

**Japan**
- **JFSA / FSA** — registered exchanges only; marketing of unregistered tokens to JP residents prohibited
- Stablecoin framework via Payment Services Act

**Vietnam**
- **SBV (Ngân Hàng Nhà Nước)** — crypto **prohibited** as a means of payment; trading is in regulatory grey zone
- Avoid framing crypto as "currency" or "payment method" in VN-targeted content
- Tax + AML rules evolving — keep current

**Hong Kong**
- **SFC** — VATP (Virtual Asset Trading Platform) licensing regime
- Retail allowed for licensed VATPs; strict standards apply

**India**
- 30% capital gains tax + 1% TDS on transfers; no clear regulator yet
- Marketing of non-INR settlements may have implications under FEMA

**Specific content categories**
- **NFT drops** — increasingly being treated as securities offerings; disclosures required.
- **Airdrop campaigns** — taxable events in many jurisdictions; "free" framing is dangerous.
- **DeFi protocols** — front-end interface vs underlying protocol; regulatory treatment differs.
- **Memecoins** — disclaimer-heavy; many jurisdictions treat as gambling/speculation; never imply investment thesis.
- **Yield products** — explicitly disclose counterparty risk, smart-contract risk, depeg risk.
- **CEX promotions** — geo-block where unlicensed; clear region-eligibility on every promo.

**Always add to the draft (verbatim)**:

> ⚠️ **Legal review + jurisdictional check required before publishing.** This draft contains crypto/web3 content. Verify token classification, claims accuracy, jurisdictional legality, paid-partnership disclosures, and audit-report references with your legal/compliance team. Confirm content is geo-targeted only at jurisdictions where it is permissible. Do not publish without sign-off.
