# Preset — SaaS

Loaded only when `preset=saas`. Layers SaaS-specific angles, hooks, trust accelerators, pricing patterns, and compliance reminders on top of the framework. Works for B2B and B2C SaaS, PLG and sales-led, vertical and horizontal tools.

---

## Niche Coverage

- **B2B SaaS** — sales-led, mid-market and enterprise (CRM, MAP, ITSM, ERP, vertical SaaS)
- **B2B PLG** — bottom-up adoption (Notion-like, Linear-like, dev tools)
- **B2C SaaS** — productivity, fitness, finance, education-tech
- **Vertical SaaS** — built for one industry (LegalTech, RetailTech, ConstructionTech)
- **Horizontal SaaS** — cross-industry tools (analytics, communication, project mgmt)
- **AI-native** — LLM/copilot products built on AI foundations
- **Dev tools / infra** — APIs, SDKs, observability, CI/CD, databases

---

## Audience Archetypes

- **Economic Buyer** (CFO, VP, CRO, CIO) — owns budget, cares about ROI, business case, security/compliance posture, contract terms.
- **Champion** (mid-level mgr, team lead) — feels the pain daily, wants to be seen as the person who solved it, will sell internally.
- **End User / IC** — uses the tool every day; cares about UX, learning curve, time saved per task.
- **Evaluator** (RevOps, IT, Procurement, Security) — gates the buy; cares about integrations, SOC 2, data residency, exit-clauses.
- **Developer** (DX-led products) — cares about API quality, docs, time-to-first-call, debugging experience, pricing fairness.
- **Founder / CEO** (small business / startup buyer) — cares about speed, simplicity, doesn't want to fly blind.

Match angle to the buyer in the seat. Mid-funnel content often sits in champion's hands; closing content speaks to economic buyer + evaluator.

## SaaS-Specific Hooks

**For B2B**
- "We replaced [tool] in 14 days. Here's what we'd do differently."
- "Stop paying for [legacy category]. The new way costs 1/10."
- "Your [team] doesn't need another tool — they need [outcome]."
- "I audited 200 [company type]'s [function]. Here's the pattern that wins."
- "If your [metric] is below [threshold], the bottleneck is almost always [system]."
- "Three reasons we ripped out [tool] and built our own. (We probably shouldn't have.)"

**For PLG**
- "Most teams discover [tool] when their first ticket gets lost."
- "The 90-second setup that replaced our 14-step onboarding doc."
- "I tried [tool] for 30 days. Here's what surprised me."

**For dev tools**
- "We rewrote the parser. Here's the perf delta."
- "If you're hitting [error], the fix is almost always [thing]."
- "Diff between v1 and v2: 84 fewer lines, 6× faster."

**For AI-native**
- "We compared [our model/agent] against GPT-4o on 200 real [tasks]."
- "Stop prompt-engineering. Use [pattern] instead."

**Visual hooks** — before/after dashboards, code diffs, real customer Slack screenshots (with permission).

## Trust Accelerators

Stack 2–3 per piece:

- **Logo wall** — must include 1–2 names the audience recognizes; vague ones hurt more than help.
- **Named customer case studies** — quote + company + role + specific metric ("cut onboarding from 14 days to 3").
- **Security badges** — SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, HIPAA, PCI-DSS, GDPR, FedRAMP. Only badge what you've actually achieved.
- **G2 / Capterra ratings** — leader badges, 4.5+ stars, recent review count.
- **Founder/operator-led posts** — "Here's what we changed last quarter and the data" outperforms brand handle posts.
- **Integration ecosystem** — visual map of supported integrations.
- **Open-source repos / public changelog** — signals shipping velocity.
- **Specific numbers from public docs** — "Pulls from 6 sources every 90 seconds," not "syncs in real time."
- **Customer count** — "Used by 12,000 teams" beats "trusted by thousands."

Avoid: borrowed logos without permission, fabricated quotes, stock-photo "team" pages.

## Pricing Patterns

- **Free tier / freemium** — best for PLG; risky for sales-led (cannibalizes).
- **Per-seat** — predictable, well-understood; can be dishonest if real value scales differently.
- **Usage-based** — fair, scales with value; harder to forecast for buyer.
- **Hybrid (per-seat + usage)** — common for AI-native; communicate clearly.
- **Annual discount anchor** — show monthly first, then "save 20% annual" as the anchor move.
- **Enterprise "talk to sales" tier** — Decoy Effect for the published tier; also genuine for >$50K deals.
- **Value Stacking on enterprise** — list everything included (premium support, SSO, SLA, dedicated CSM, custom contracts).
- **Free trial vs reverse-trial** — reverse-trial (start in paid, downgrade later) outperforms standard free trial for most B2B.
- **Reverse-billing month** — "Pay only when you've sent your first [outcome]."
- **Per-event pricing** for AI products — be transparent about variable cost; show calculator on pricing page.
- **No charm pricing on enterprise** — round numbers signal confidence ("$2,000/mo" beats "$1,999").

## Voice Tone Defaults

- **Confident Expert** — primary, especially B2B mid-market and enterprise
- **Builder-to-Builder** — for developer tools, infra, AI-native (avoid marketing-speak entirely)
- **Trusted Advisor** — for high-stakes verticals (HealthTech SaaS, FinTech SaaS, GovTech SaaS)
- **Inspirational Leader** — for category-defining founders, only when backed by traction

Avoid: corporate-fluffy ("seamless," "best-in-class"), emoji-bullet ✅✅✅ posts, AI-template prose ("In today's fast-paced…").

## Platform Fits

- **LinkedIn** (primary for most B2B SaaS) — exec/founder voice, document carousels, anti-conventional takes
- **X/Twitter** — developer + founder voice, build-in-public, real-time commentary, threads
- **YouTube** — long-form demos, customer interviews, technical deep-dives, comparison videos
- **Email** — nurture sequences, product launches, trial-to-paid lifecycle
- **Blog/SEO** — BOFU comparison ("X vs Y"), how-to tutorials, glossary, "alternatives to" pages
- **Reddit** — niche subs (r/devops, r/ProductManagement, r/sysadmin) — value-first only, no hard sell
- **Podcasts** — long-form thought leadership, customer interviews
- **Slack/Discord communities** — for developer tools and PLG products

Avoid: TikTok and Instagram for most B2B SaaS (waste); exception: AI-native consumer-feeling tools (Notion, Cursor, ElevenLabs) where founder personality drives reach.

## Compliance Reminders

**Always surface these with the draft:**

- **Customer quotes** — must be real, attributed with permission. Fabricating quotes is illegal in most jurisdictions and will torch credibility on discovery.
- **Logo permission** — using a customer logo on your site usually requires explicit permission; assume yes only if it's in the contract.
- **Security/compliance claims** — only badge what you've achieved (SOC 2 Type II != SOC 2 Type I). Misleading badges are FTC-actionable.
- **Data residency claims** ("EU data stays in EU") — must be exact and current as you scale infrastructure.
- **GDPR / CCPA / India DPDP** — privacy claims and DSAR processes must match your actual implementation.
- **AI / LLM-specific** — disclose AI use in customer communications where required (EU AI Act, state AI disclosure laws); be careful about claims of "no hallucinations" or "100% accurate."
- **Comparison advertising** — naming a competitor and claiming superiority requires defensible evidence (Lanham Act in US, Trade Practices laws in EU/UK/AU).
- **"Free forever"** — if you reserve the right to change terms, say so; deceptive "forever" claims are FTC-actionable.

**Always add to the draft**: "Note: validate any customer quote/logo/security claim with the relevant team before publishing."
