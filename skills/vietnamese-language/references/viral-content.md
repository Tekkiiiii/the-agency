## Hook Mechanics Specific to Vietnamese Audience

### What VN audiences respond to distinctly from global
- Confessional opening ("Thú thật là...") outperforms curiosity-gap in 18–35 female
- Specific Vietnamese numbers (not rounded) signal authenticity
- Community membership hooks ("Ai là dân [miền/nghề/sở thích]?") drive strong saves
- Shame-to-pride arc (từ xấu hổ đến tự hào): high-performing in beauty/education
- Family connection hooks outperform individual-achievement for 30+ demographics

### Pattern: Pain + Embarrassment + Solution
1. Name a situation everyone experienced but no one admits
2. Validate the embarrassment ("Không có gì xấu hổ")
3. Reveal the simple fix

## Slang Lifecycle: TV → TikTok → Cross-Platform

- Phase 1 (Origin): TV shows, celebrities, reality shows coin the term
- Phase 2 (TikTok): content creators remix into hooks
- Phase 3 (Cross-platform): enters FB comments, IG captions, brand copy
- Phase 4 (Saturation): brands using it = signal it's expiring for Gen Z
- Brands should enter at Phase 2-3 only; Phase 4 = cringe

## Meme Culture and Format Conventions

- Vietnamese memes riff on local film dialogue (Đất Rừng Phương Nam, Kính Vạn Hoa era)
- Format: image macro — top line sets up, bottom line subverts
- "Hội chứng [X]" format: naming relatable behavior as fake syndrome
- Self-deprecating humor > punching-at-others for brand memes

## Challenge/Trend Participation Language

### Joining
- "Mình cũng thử challenge [tên] xem sao"
- "Không thể cưỡng lại trend này"
- Tag @original creator + #challenge hashtag

### Launching branded challenges
- Verb-based names outperform noun-based
- Seed with first 10 UGC participants
- Prize language: avoid appearing as paid promotion

## Emotional Trigger Vocabulary

Distinct from vi.md power words (which focus on conversion) — these signal high-share content:
- Nostalgia: tuổi thơ, ngày xưa, hồi nhỏ, ký ức
- Pride: tự hào, người Việt, made in Vietnam
- Belonging: hội mình, đồng đội, gia đình
- Empathy: ai cũng có lúc, mình cũng vậy, không chỉ mình bạn
- Hope: sẽ ổn thôi, mọi thứ sẽ tốt hơn

## Patriotic/Cultural Identity Hooks

- "Made in Vietnam" / "Thuần Việt" positioning language
- Appropriate vs appropriative use of cultural symbols (nón lá, áo dài, phở)
- National holiday content: tôn trọng vs marketing-washing
- Rule: authentic origin, not performance — brands must have genuine Vietnamese connection

## Seasonal Viral Patterns

### Tết (January/February)
- Pre-Tết viral window: 3 weeks before (nostalgia peaks)
- Tết Day 1–3: family content dominates; brand content pauses
- Post-Tết (Day 4–14): "back to normal" content wins

### Tết Trung Thu (September/October)
- Child-centric content; mooncake marketing; lantern festival UGC

### Other high-viral windows
- 8/3 (Int'l Women's Day): empowerment > celebration
- 20/10 (VN Women's Day): more local, more intimate
- 20/11 (Teachers' Day): gratitude content
- 1/6 (Children's Day): family content
- 2/9 (National Day): patriotic but not political
