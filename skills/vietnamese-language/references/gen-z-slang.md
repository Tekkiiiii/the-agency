## Formation Patterns

Understanding HOW Vietnamese Gen Z slang forms lets you generalize to new terms.

### Pattern 1: Number Substitution
Vietnamese numerals replace syllables with similar sounds:
- 4 = "tư" or sounds like English "for"
- 7 = "bảy" → "7749 kiểu" = vô số (countless)
- "2k" = hai ngàn = born around 2000 (generation marker)
- "9x" = born in the 90s (generation marker)
- "8386" = phát tài phát lộc (prosperity wish)
- Detection: number where a syllable would go → check phonetic equivalence

### Pattern 2: English Loanword Integration
- Single-syllable English words: used as-is ("vibe", "flex", "ship", "chill")
- Multi-syllable: often abbreviated ("aesthetic" → sometimes just "aes")
- Verb integration: English verb + rồi/nha/đi as particles ("flex rồi", "chill đi")
- Adjective integration: English adj + lắm/quá ("cute lắm", "chill quá")
- Fully integrated (use without marking): vibe, flex, chill, slay, trend, cute, okay, review, deal, sale
- Still marked as foreign (use with awareness): aesthetic, glow up, era, main character

### Pattern 3: Onomatopoeic Repetition
- Base word + rhyme/near-rhyme variation: "xịn xò" (very cool), "lầy lội" (irreverently funny)
- Common final elements: -ò, -ịu, -iu, -ộn
- This pattern signals playfulness and informality

### Pattern 4: Abbreviation and Initialism
Vietnamese text-speak:
- "k" = không (no); "dc" = được (can/OK); "r" = rồi (already/done)
- "bc" = báo cáo (report); "nx" = nữa (more/again)
- "ns" = nói sao (what to say); "bt" = bình thường (normal)
- These are stable, not trending — used across all platforms

### Pattern 5: Semantic Shift from TV/Meme Origin
- A common word gains new meaning from a viral clip or meme
- Original meaning remains; new meaning is layered (context-dependent)
- Example: "sít rịt" (2025) — originally "very close/tight" → viral meaning "rare/exceptional"
- Detection: sudden usage spike of a common word in unusual context

## Shelf Life Indicators

- **Evergreen**: 3+ years of use, embedded in general VN internet language. Safe for brands.
- **Trending**: 6–18 months peak, ascending or plateauing. Use if brand targets youth.
- **Expiring**: past peak. When a term appears in mainstream media headlines, government communications, or TV show titles — it has left Gen Z.
- Rule: brands using Expiring-tier terms = "đã thua" (already lost)

## Code-Switching Norms

- Switch happens at phrase boundary, not mid-word
- English nouns: common; English verbs: less common but growing
- Heavy code-switching = very informal register
- All-Vietnamese = either formal or self-consciously "thuần Việt"
- Brand voice: match code-switching level of audience segment
