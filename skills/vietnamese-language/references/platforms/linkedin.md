# LinkedIn VN — Cultural Behavior Patterns

> Supplements content-creator/references/platforms.md (format specs).
> For formal Vietnamese conventions → references/formal-documents.md.

## Professional Vietnamese Conventions

- Full-name usage: Vietnamese LinkedIn uses full name (Nguyễn Văn A), not just first name
- Self-introduction: title + company + years + what you share — differs from Western "I help X do Y"
- Achievement framing: indirect required — "Team mình đã đạt được..." (Our team achieved...) not "Tôi đã..."
- Personal stories acceptable but must tie to professional insight — pure personal = Facebook spillover

## Hierarchy and Deference in Digital Context

- Mentioning superiors: always use title ("Anh/Chị Giám đốc [tên]"), never first name alone
- Tagging: Ông/Bà + full title when tagging executives or senior professionals
- Comment register: formal even if your own posts are casual when engaging with a superior's post
- Never publicly disagree with a superior's post — redirect to DM

## B2B Register

- Thought leadership: structured analysis with numbered takeaways outperforms hot-take opinions
- Formality: "Anh/chị" default, "bạn" only in explicitly peer communities
- Lead generation CTAs: "Anh chị quan tâm có thể inbox mình" — soft, never "Book a call now"
- Industry jargon: kept in English (ROAS, SaaS, B2B) — VN LinkedIn expects technical English

## Job Posting Language

- Structure: Company intro → Role → Requirements → Benefits → Application method
- Inclusive language: "Không phân biệt giới tính, độ tuổi"
- Salary: growing transparency — "Mức lương: thương lượng" or specific range
- Application CTA: "Gửi CV qua email [X] hoặc inbox trực tiếp"

## VN LinkedIn vs Global LinkedIn

- What transfers: professional tone, thought leadership format, industry analysis
- What doesn't: aggressive self-promotion, "I'm excited to announce" formula (feels performative in VN), confrontational hot takes
