# Protocol — Marketing → Content Creation Handoff

Marketing owns **strategy**. Content Creation owns **execution**. This protocol governs how Marketing builds and delivers strategic briefs to the CCO.

## Strategic Brief Format

Every content request to Content Creation includes:
- **What** to create — content type, format, topic, key messages
- **Who** to target — audience segment, persona, buyer stage (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU)
- **When** to publish — editorial calendar, campaign timeline, launch dates
- **Where** to distribute — channel, platform, placement
- **Why** this matters — campaign objective, KPI, success metric
- **Brand context** — voice guidelines, competitor positioning, market insights

## Flow

1. Marketing Lead builds strategic brief using the format above
2. Marketing Lead sends brief to CCO (Content Creation Lead)
3. CCO routes to the right writer based on format/platform
4. Content Creation produces and returns production-ready artifact with quality gate certification
5. Marketing Lead reviews for strategic alignment
6. Marketing owns publishing, distribution, engagement, and performance tracking
7. Marketing shares performance data back to Content Creation (feedback loop)

## Feedback Loop

After distribution, share performance data (engagement, CTR, conversion, audience response) back to Content Creation. Better briefs and better data produce better content.

## Boundaries

- Marketing never writes final copy — Content Creation owns craft and editorial quality
- Content Creation never publishes directly — Marketing owns distribution and publishing
- Strategy disputes escalate to council-chair (parent AI)
